Introduction to Pad Printing

What is Pad Printing?

Pad printing (also reffered to as tampography) is a manufacturing printing process that transforms a 2-dimensional image onto a 3-dimensional body by actuating an applicator between two offsets. The most common industies include: medical, automotive, apparel, and electronics!

Cycle Overview

  • I: Home Position
    • The ink cup (inverted to trap the ink) rests over an etched out image on the printing plate (clich√©).
  • II: Leave Home Position
    • The ink cup is linearly actuated away from the ink-filled etched image. The ink becomes tacky due to the exposure to air which aids its adhesive properties.
  • III: Actuate Transfer Pad Downward
    • The transfer pad is linearly actuated downward onto the etching momentarily where the ink adheres to the pad surface.
  • IV: Actuate Transfer Pad Upward
    • The transfer pad is linearly actuated upward. A negligible amount of ink is left in the ethcing.
  • V: Actuate Transfer Pad and Ink Cup
    • As the transfer pad is actuated over the substrate, the ink cup is actuated back over the etching to prepare for the next cycle.
  • VI: Actuate Transfer Pad Downward
    • The transfer pad is actuated downward onto the subtrate such that the pressure is great enough to completely transfer the image and conform to the substrate's geometry.

Dual Pad Printing

At Cadence, pad printing is optimized by using the PS-2200 2-color pad printer in order to keep up with demand. This is necessary because the Stryker Torque Wrench requires a mirrored image on the other half of the external sleeve. With the addition of dual sided pad printing, labor is essentially cut in half since the operator does not have to print one side at a time.
